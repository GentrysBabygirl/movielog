Latest Updates

  1. February 9, 2015

    Curse of the Crimson Altar (1968)

    Directed by Vernon Sewell. Starring Boris Karloff, Christopher Lee, and Mark Eden.

    The Curse of the Crimson Altar (The Crimson Cult here in the USA) can't decide how seriously it wants to take itself.

  2. February 6, 2015

    The Town That Dreaded Sundown (2014)

    Directed by Alfonso Gomez-Rejon. Starring Addison Timlin, Veronica Cartwright, and Anthony Anderson.

    The Town That Dreaded Sundown may be the first rural giallo.

  3. February 4, 2015

    The Big Steal (1949)

    Directed by Don Siegel. Starring Robert Mitchum, Jane Greer, and William Bendix.

    The Big Steal takes a B-movie plot and elevates it to near greatness thanks to a well-crafted script peppered with crackling dialog.

  4. January 30, 2015

    Rasputin: The Mad Monk (1966)

    Directed by Don Sharp. Starring Christopher Lee, Barbara Shelley, and Richard Pasco.

    Rasputin spends too much time drinking and dancing and not enough time chopping off hands.

  5. January 29, 2015

    The Devil Rides Out (1968)

    Directed by Terence Fisher. Starring Christopher Lee, Charles Gray, and Nike Arrighi.

    Some films have no business working as well as they do. The Devil Rides Out is one such film.

  6. January 25, 2015

    The One I Love (2014)

    Directed by Charlie McDowell. Starring Mark Duplass, Elisabeth Moss, and Ted Danson.

    Warning: There be spoilers here. It's not possible to talk about The One I Love in a meaningful way without them. But it’s a movie best seen with an open mind. So I encourage you, if you haven't seen it, to do so. It's not perfect, but the first two acts are brilliant and it's original enough to warrant a look. Go ahead, I'll wait.

  7. January 17, 2015

    The Angry Hills (1959)

    Directed by Robert Aldrich. Starring Robert Mitchum, Stanley Baker, and Elisabeth Müller.

    In the end, The Angry Hills isn't really a movie at all. Instead where given the beginning of one movie, the middle of another, and the end of yet another.

  8. January 15, 2015

    Rachel and the Stranger (1948)

    Directed by Norman Foster. Starring Loretta Young, William Holden, and Robert Mitchum.

    There's a kernel of a good story here, and the three leads are more than capable of pulling it off. But the hackneyed melodramatic fable they're given to work with is frustrating at best, and annoying at worst.

  9. January 14, 2015

    Locke (2013)

    Directed by Steven Knight. Starring Tom Hardy, Olivia Colman, and Ruth Wilson.

    Locke is not a great film, but it is a good one. If you're on the fence about seeing it, do so. Don't read any further, as we're going to talk about plot and this movie works best the less you know going in.

  10. January 10, 2015

    She Couldn't Say No (1954)

    Directed by Lloyd Bacon. Starring Robert Mitchum, Jean Simmons, and Arthur Hunnicutt.

    Had it dared to be as provocative as it's title, She Couldn't Say No might have been something special. As is, it’s not a bad film, but it is something of a let-down.
