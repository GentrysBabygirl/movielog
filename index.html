Latest Updates

  1. January 15, 2015

    Rachel and the Stranger (1948)

    Directed by Norman Foster. Starring Loretta Young, William Holden, and Robert Mitchum.

    There's a kernel of a good story here, and the three leads are more than capable of pulling it off. But the hackneyed melodramatic fable they're given to work with is frustrating at best, and annoying at worst.

  2. January 14, 2015

    Locke (2013)

    Directed by Steven Knight. Starring Tom Hardy, Olivia Colman, and Ruth Wilson.

    Locke is not a great film, but it is a good one. If you're on the fence about seeing it, do so. Don't read any further, as we're going to talk about plot and this movie works best the less you know going in.

  3. January 10, 2015

    She Couldn't Say No (1954)

    Directed by Lloyd Bacon. Starring Robert Mitchum, Jean Simmons, and Arthur Hunnicutt.

    Had it dared to be as provocative as it's title, She Couldn't Say No might have been something special. As is, it’s not a bad film, but it is something of a let-down.

  4. January 7, 2015

    Friday the 13th Part VIII: Jason Takes Manhattan (1989)

    Directed by Rob Hedden. Starring Todd Caldecott, Tiffany Paulsen, and Tim Mirkovich.

    Friday the 13th Part VIII: Jason Takes Manhattan is more disappointing than awful. Aside from a change of scenery, it doesn’t offer anything we haven’t seen done better before.

  5. January 5, 2015

    Friday the 13th Part VII: The New Blood (1988)

    Directed by John Carl Buechler. Starring Jennifer Banko, John Otrin, and Susan Blu.

    In Friday the 13th Part VII: The New Blood, it’s the audience, not Jason’s quarry that suffers.

  6. January 1, 2015

    Jason Lives: Friday the 13th Part VI (1986)

    Directed by Tom McLoughlin. Starring Thom Mathews, Jennifer Cooke, and David Kagen.

    Jason Lives: Friday the 13th Part VI isn’t an awful movie. The depiction of Jason is great, and the fourth-wall humor is a nice touch. But the forced melodrama and bland supporting cast drag things down.

  7. December 31, 2014

    Friday the 13th: A New Beginning (1985)

    Directed by Danny Steinmann. Starring Melanie Kinnaman, John Shepherd, and Shavar Ross.

    Time has been kind to Friday the 13th: A New Beginning. Knowing that the franchise rights itself, fans can appreciate this entry in a so-bad-it’s-good kind of way.

  8. December 29, 2014

    Horror of Dracula (1958)

    Directed by Terence Fisher. Starring Peter Cushing, Christopher Lee, and Michael Gough.

    Howard Hawks once said that a good movie consisted of "three great scenes and no bad ones." Hammer's first entry in its Dracula franchise gives us exactly that.

  9. December 25, 2014

    Interstellar (2014)

    Directed by Christopher Nolan. Starring Ellen Burstyn, Matthew McConaughey, and Mackenzie Foy.

    It’s tempting to say that Interstellar’s reach exceeded its grasp. That it tried and failed. I don’t think it tried at all.
